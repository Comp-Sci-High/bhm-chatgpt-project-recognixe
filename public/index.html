Martin Luther King Jr.

Fun Fact: MLK was the youngest person to win the Nobel Peace Prize in 1964.

Martin Luther King Jr. was a civil rights leader who fought for racial equality through nonviolent resistance, inspiring millions worldwide.

Harriet Tubman

Fun Fact: Harriet Tubman helped free over 70 enslaved people through the Underground Railroad.

Harriet Tubman was an abolitionist and political activist who led numerous enslaved people to freedom and later assisted in the women's suffrage movement.

Malcolm X

Fun Fact: Malcolm X was an avid reader and educated himself in prison by copying the entire dictionary.

Malcolm X was a prominent figure in the civil rights movement, advocating for Black empowerment and self-determination.

Maya Angelou

Fun Fact: Maya Angelou was the first African American woman to have her screenplay produced as a film.

Maya Angelou was a poet, singer, and civil rights activist known for her autobiographical works that uplifted the Black community.
